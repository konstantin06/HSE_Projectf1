Conclusion the starting position really has a stronger effect on the finishing position on the track in Monaco. But in turn, on the track in Belgium, the fastest lap time has a stronger effect on the finishing position than in Monaco. This is due to the peculiarities of the tracks. The track in Monaco is narrow and slow, with a huge number of turns, it is difficult to overtake there, so the closer you start to pole position, the more likely you are to finish higher in the final table. In Belgium, on the contrary, the track is high-speed and overtaking, so the starting position plays a lesser role than in Monaco. But the fastest lap time affects the finishing position more strongly in Belgium. Also, due to the fact that the track in Belgium is faster, it can be observed that the higher the maximum speed of the car, the higher its finishing position (feedback).
